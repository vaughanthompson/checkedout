Frequently Asked Questions

Look for answers to common questions below. If you can't find what you're looking for please get in touch.

 
 

Buyers


Why should I get a Building Inspection Report before I place an offer?

You need to get the property checked out first so you can decide if you want to make an offer, and if that offer is subject to anything discovered during a due diligence process.

Unfortunately the current model and process is broken. Reports are so costly and buyers are so desperate to get their offer accepted quickly, the due diligence usually waits until buyers are in the conditional period.

How does a CheckedOut MSIP report fix the broken house purchase process?

The current broken house purchase process works like this:

  1. You view and you want the property, you might even get to see a free Vendor's Building Report (but you know you can't trust and can't rely on it)
  2. You place an offer, it's accepted, and you now have made a commitment and signed a legally binding contract
  3. You then begin your due diligence and start checking out the property. Often it's now too late to set the contract aside without incurring additional costs or legal fees.

How CheckedOut fixes this

It's a model that provides the most important information about your potential new home BEFORE you make an offer and before you sign a contract.

Our MSIP reports guarantee you get trustworthy, independent information before you've signed. You know for sure if there are any building issues and you can make an informed decision on how to proceed with your offer.

What Due Diligence do I need?

An MSIP report via CheckedOut, together with LIM, Title checks and any other documentation recommended by your legal or financial advisor/s.

MSIP-based Building Reports are cost effective, trusted, and reliable. Eliminate the need to not only put yourself through all the Due Diligence effort, costing upwards of $2000- but you also risk 2 or 3 weeks being in limbo and out of the buyer's market.

Why can I trust and rely on MSIP reports, but I can't trust a vendors building report?

MSIP building reports can be verified as impartial, having been independently sourced, inspected and written by an inspector who has NO affiliation with the owner or real estate agent selling the home.

What are the problems with current vendor building reports?

Unregulated. They are not regulated and not subject to any legislation requiring impartiality. Therefore anyone can inspect a property, offer their opinion and provide a written building report for the vendor, whether they are suitably qualified or not!

Not impartial. Often the inspector providing vendor based reports is acquainted to the vendor or agent. In many cases this 'inspector' is a builder NOT a specialized building inspector- producing what is known in the real estate industry as "soft building reports."

A vendor sourced report that isn't subject to an independence process such as ours can- even unknowingly, have vendor bias attached to it.

No insurance. The report is in the vendor's name therefore the 'privy of contract' exists only between them and the inspector. No ability for recourse exists for insurance claims against the inspector by buyers using this type of report.

Does the Real Estate Authority (REA*) not permit or disallow soft building inspections?

Unfortunately, the current lack of regulation around building inspections, means soft building inspections remain a common practice for vendor building reports, and as a result the REA steps in and tells Real Estate Agents what to do and what to say.

*The REA are the Independent Government agency that regulates the New Zealand Real Estate Industry - they issue Agents their licences and tell Agents what they must do and how they practice. The Building Inspection Industry is not regulated. This means Agents must follow the rules and provide the best advice but there are no rules for Inspectors.

What do the REA advise Real Estate Agents in regards to vendor building reports?

The REA requires all real estate agents to tell potential homebuyers when there is a vendors building report available. They recommend the potential buyer order & book their own building inspection report on this same property.

Are CheckedOut MSIP building reports insurable?

YES! Thanks to our independent approach, inspectors are willing and able to transfer buyer's names (readdress) onto reports to enable you to seek insurance cover for the report contents.

Note: You will have to check with your respective bank and insurer, but most banks will accept a building report if they can verify they are independent building reports. Banks won't generally accept the vendor's building report for the exact reasons and will require you to book your own building inspection.

How can I trust and rely on an MSIP building report that has been paid for by the vendor. Isn't it still an untrustworthy report?

No. Because an MSIP report follows a process that eliminates the concerns regarding impartiality, un-insurability and bias. See how it works.
